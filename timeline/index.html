Timeline of History

› Timeline
Year: Category:
January 20, 2025
Inauguration
Inauguration as 47th President
Donald J. Trump was sworn in as the 47th President of the United States, becoming the first president in American history to serve two non-consecutive terms since Grover Cleveland. The ceremony was held at the U.S. Capitol.
January 20, 2025
Executive Order
DOGE Established via EO 14158
The Department of Government Efficiency was established by Executive Order 14158. Elon Musk was appointed as a Special Government Employee to lead the initiative, tasked with modernizing federal operations and identifying spending reductions.
January 20, 2025
Presidential Action
Pardons for January 6th Individuals
President Trump signed pardons for approximately 1,500 individuals who had been charged or convicted in connection with the events of January 6, 2021, fulfilling a campaign commitment.
January 20, 2025
Foreign Policy
Withdrew from Paris Climate Accord
On Inauguration Day, President Trump signed the executive order to withdraw the United States from the Paris Climate Accord for the second time, asserting that the agreement placed undue burdens on American energy and industry.
January 20, 2025
Foreign Policy
Withdrew from World Health Organization
The United States formally began withdrawal from the World Health Organization, citing failures of transparency and accountability during the COVID-19 pandemic and the organization's structural deference to member state interests over global health truth.
January 20, 2025
Executive Order
Ended Federal DEI Programs
An executive order signed on Inauguration Day terminated all Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion programs, mandates, and offices across all federal agencies, restoring merit-based principles to the federal workforce.
January 20, 2025
National Security
National Emergency at Southern Border
President Trump declared a national emergency at the U.S.-Mexico southern border, unlocking emergency powers and federal resources to deploy National Guard troops and accelerate enforcement operations.
January 20, 2025
Executive Order
"Unleashing American Energy" EO Signed
A sweeping energy executive order directed federal agencies to expedite permits for oil, gas, coal, and nuclear energy production, reversing regulatory barriers and declaring American energy dominance a national security priority.
March 4, 2025
Legislation
Laken Riley Act Signed into Law
The Laken Riley Act — the first legislation signed in the second term — mandated detention of undocumented individuals charged with burglary, theft, or violent offenses. It was named in honor of Laken Riley, a Georgia nursing student killed by an illegal immigrant in 2024.
March 15, 2025
Legislation
Continuing Appropriations Bill Enacted
A continuing resolution to fund the federal government was enacted, avoiding a government shutdown and providing operating continuity while the administration pursued its broader budget reform agenda through DOGE.
April 2, 2025
Economic Policy
"Liberation Day" — Global Tariff Announcement
President Trump announced a 10% baseline tariff on imports from nearly all countries, accompanied by country-specific reciprocal tariffs designed to correct decades of unfair trade practices. The announcement marked the most significant restructuring of U.S. trade policy in the post-war era.
April 9, 2025
Economic Policy
90-Day Pause on Reciprocal Tariffs
A 90-day pause on country-specific reciprocal tariffs was announced, providing a negotiating window for bilateral trade agreements with key partners including the EU, UK, Japan, South Korea, and others, while the baseline 10% tariff remained in effect.
September 25, 2025
Technology
TikTok Saved via Executive Order
An executive order provided a legal framework to preserve TikTok's operations in the United States while addressing national security concerns related to its Chinese ownership, protecting American users' access while safeguarding sensitive data.
September 30, 2025
Technology
AI Cancer Research EO Signed
An executive order directed federal agencies to harness artificial intelligence to accelerate research into cures for pediatric cancer — combining technological innovation with a compassionate national health priority.
October 2025
Foreign Policy
Israel–Hamas Ceasefire Secured
After sustained diplomatic engagement by the Trump administration, a ceasefire between Israel and Hamas was successfully negotiated. All remaining hostages held in Gaza were returned — a historic humanitarian achievement that marked the resolution of an eighteen-month conflict.
November 2025
Domestic Reform
Department of Education Restructuring
The process of restructuring and winding down the federal Department of Education was formally announced, with K-12 and higher education functions to be transferred to individual states, returning educational authority to communities.
December 11, 2025
Technology
AI National Policy Framework EO Signed
An executive order established the United States' comprehensive national AI policy framework, positioning America as the global leader in artificial intelligence development, safety standards, and innovation governance.
December 15, 2025
National Security
Fentanyl Designated as Weapon of Mass Destruction
President Trump signed an executive order formally designating fentanyl as a Weapon of Mass Destruction, unlocking additional law enforcement, military, and diplomatic tools to combat the drug's flow into the United States from foreign sources.
December 18, 2025
Technology
American Space Superiority EO Signed
The "Ensuring American Space Superiority" executive order established a national framework for maintaining U.S. dominance in space exploration, satellite infrastructure, and space-based national defense systems.
January 20, 2026
Anniversary
One-Year Anniversary — 243 Executive Orders Signed
On the one-year anniversary of his inauguration, President Trump marked 243 executive orders signed — a historic high. The administration cited transformational progress across border security, economic policy, federal efficiency, and foreign diplomacy.
